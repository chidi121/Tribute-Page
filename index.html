Kobe Bryant

The man who lived a life filled with legacy

Here's a time line of Kobe Bryant life:

  • 1978 - in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania

  • 1984 - after ending his NBA career, the elder Bryant took the family to Italy,
    where he played in the Italian League.

  • 1991 - Returned to Philadelphia. Bryant joined the Lower Merion High School basketball team,
    leading it to the state championships four years in a row.

  • 1996 - He was selected by the Charlotte Hornets with the 13th overall pick of the 1996
    NBA draft and was subsequently traded to the Los Angeles Lakers.

  • 1998 - In his second season with the Lakers, Bryant was voted a starter for the 1998
    All-Star Game, becoming the youngest All-Star in NBA history at 19.

  • 2006 - He scored 81 points against the Toronto Raptors in January.
    the second-highest single-game mark in NBA history, and led the league in scoring that year and the next.

  • 2008 - Bryant was named Most Valuable Player and carried his team to the NBA Finals,
    where they lost to the Boston Celtics.

  • 2009 - the Lakers beat the Orlando Magic to win the championship.

  • 2013 - Suffered a torn Achilles tendon

  • 2015 - he announced that he would retire at the end of the season.

  • 2016 - Bryant dazzled a sold-out crowd at the Staples Center and fans everywhere
    in the last game of his career, scoring 60 points and leading the Lakers to a win against the Utah Jazz.
    It was Bryant’s sixth 60-point game of his career.

  • 2020 - He died tragically in a helicopter crash.
